Respect does not mean deference.

Respect is treating another person with basic dignity. It is not obedience, servitude, or placing someone above yourself simply because they expect it.

Sometimes respect means listening. Sometimes it means helping. Sometimes it means disagreeing, correcting, or taking a stand.

Respect should flow both ways. Not everyone deserves your giving, your submission, or your trust. But there is a base standard of human dignity, and confusing that with deference is where the rot begins.
